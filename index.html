The great knitting witch Elizabeth Zimmermann isn't known for hand-holding, step-by-step patterns that hawk $200 in wool and silk mohair, but insightful, wry, no-bullshit prose guidance.

For her sweaters—seamed and seamless, top down and bottom-up—she repeatedly returned to approximate percentages known as "Elizabeth's Percentage System." All anyone needs to knit a sweater is horizontal stitch gauge and bust circumference.
